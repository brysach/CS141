\section{Find Minimum Value}
To look for the minimum value of a function $f(x)$, where $x \in (1, 2)$, basically, we look for the \var{middle} point between the endpoitns of the interval. Then, my algorithm determines the behavior of $f(x)$ around \var{middle}. If $f(x)$ decreases by moving \var{tol} along $x$-axis either left or right, then the interval to look in that minimum value of $f(x)$ changes from $\pm$\var{tol} to the other closest endpoint of the interval. The algorithm keeps iterating until the distance from the endpoints of the interval along $x$-axis is $\le$ \var{tol}. In other words, let $d = |x_i - x_j|$ be the distance of the two endpoints of the interval where the minimum point of $f(x)$ sits for all $j \le i$, so the algorithm stops when $d \le$\var{tol}.

Notice to calculate \var{middle}, we divide $d$ by half. So, let $i$ be the number of times we divide $d$ until $d\le$\var{tol}. When found the minimum value of $f(x)$, the distance would be:
\begin{align*}
    \frac{d}{2^i} & \le \text{\var{tol}}                \\
    2^i           & \ge \frac{d}{\text{\var{tol}}}      \\
    \log 2^i      & \ge \log \frac{d}{\text{\var{tol}}} \\
    i             & \ge \log \frac{d}{\text{\var{tol}}}
\end{align*}
Thus, my algorithm performs $\Omega\left(\log \frac{d}{\text{\var{tol}}}\right)$ times.
